# Self Multiplexer

> **Your data. Your terms. Their turn to pay.**

The deal the internet quietly made with you is hard to defend once it's said plainly: hand over everything about yourself, for free, to companies that resell it, and call the surveillance a "personalized experience." Self Multiplexer exists to flip that deal on its head. One identity that you own, from which you share precisely what you choose, with exactly whom you choose — and any business that wants you as a customer can pay for the privilege of your attention instead of taking your data for nothing.

The model is distributed by design. Rather than a single profile pooled in someone else's database, you hold one identity and project tailored faces of it outward — a different persona, a different contact point, a different slice of data for each place you deal with. What you share is deliberate, every disclosure is auditable, and access you grant is access you can revoke, instantly and completely.

This is the internet as it was supposed to work: ownership in your hands, not theirs; consent that means something; and a relationship between people and platforms that finally runs in the right direction.
